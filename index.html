Research / Non-Saccharomyces

2026 Study: Schizosaccharomyces japonicus Polymorphism Expands Non-Saccharomyces Toolkit for Winemaking

A 2026 ScienceDirect paper mapping Schizosaccharomyces japonicus polymorphism identifies new strain diversity in a yeast species already commercially used for tartrate stability and malic acid deacidification in heat-processed musts. The research highlights that S. japonicus strains vary significantly in their aroma, acid management, and ethanol tolerance profiles — creating selection opportunities for winemakers managing the high-acid, high-solids conditions common after thermovinification or flash détente.
💡 What this means
After thermovinification, musts often carry elevated malic acid loads and altered microbial dynamics. Schizosaccharomyces species metabolize malic acid directly and can improve tartrate stability without cold stabilization — but strain selection matters enormously. This study broadens what the tool can do for specific varietals and processing styles.
✅ Your takeaway
If Enartis carries Schizosaccharomyces-based tools or deacidification programs for heat-extracted musts, this peer-reviewed study gives you scientific backing for the conversation. For accounts doing any level of heat extraction in warm-vintage years, ask what they're currently doing for malic acid management post-processing — then bring the data.

✅ Your takeaway

MDPI Deep Learning Study Validates YOLOv7 for Real-Time Grape Maturity Assessment in the Vineyard

A new paper in MDPI Sensors presents a validated deep learning approach using YOLOv7 to assess grape maturity in real time from vineyard imagery, achieving classification accuracy sufficient for harvest timing decisions.
