Pellenc's Flash Détente: How Rapid Heat-Vacuum Processing Is Reshaping Wine Profiles for International Markets

Pellenc's Flash Détente thermovinification system rapidly heats grapes to high temperatures and immediately cools them under vacuum, extracting color and flavor compounds before fermentation begins. The result is wines that are fruitier, rounder, and more structurally approachable — characteristics aligned with demand patterns in high-growth global export markets. The technology allows winemakers to meaningfully modify wine profiles without compromising varietal character, and is increasingly used to optimize blending lots and improve consistency across vintages.
💡 What this means
Flash Détente creates a chemically distinct must — elevated temperature kills native microflora, alters polysaccharide structure, and changes the enzyme substrate significantly. Winemakers adopting this technology for the first time often discover their standard input programs — yeasts, enzymes, nutrients — don't perform the same way in heat-extracted musts. The fermentation input program has to be rebuilt around the must chemistry, not borrowed from conventional workflows.
✅ Amy's sales angle
Flash Détente is an entry point into a technical conversation most reps never initiate. Ask your red wine accounts: "Have you experimented with any heat extraction techniques?" If yes, probe their enzyme and nutrient program — there's a high probability they're running conventional protocols on unconventional musts. Enartis's technical team can help build a proper Flash Détente-specific program, and that expertise is a genuine differentiator when the account is already committed to the technology.
